# Exercise 1: Understanding Quality Assurance

## Purpose

These exercises help you internalize what Quality Assurance really means, beyond just "testing." The goal is to develop a **quality mindset** that guides all your testing decisions.

---

## Exercise 1.1: Quality in Your Life

### Task

Think about software you use daily (phone apps, websites, tools):

1. **Identify Quality Software**:
   - Pick 2-3 apps you consider "high quality"
   - What makes them quality?
   - List specific characteristics

2. **Identify Poor Quality Software**:
   - Pick 1-2 apps that frustrate you
   - What makes them poor quality?
   - What's missing or broken?

3. **Compare**:
   - What's the difference?
   - Is it just "bugs" or something more?

### Reflection

- What dimensions of quality matter most to you as a user?
- How does this relate to ISO 25010 quality characteristics?
- What does this tell you about what QA should focus on?

---

## Exercise 1.2: QA vs QC in Practice

### Task

Think about a project you've worked on (or imagine one):

1. **Quality Control Activities**:
   - What testing/inspection happened?
   - When did it happen? (After development?)
   - What defects were found?

2. **Quality Assurance Activities**:
   - What processes ensured quality?
   - What happened during development?
   - How were defects prevented?

3. **Compare**:
   - Which was more effective?
   - What would have happened with only QC?
   - What would have happened with only QA?

### Reflection

- How does QA differ from QC in practice?
- Why is QA (prevention) often more valuable than QC (detection)?
- How can you shift from QC to QA mindset?

---

## Exercise 1.3: QA vs Testing

### Task

List all activities a QA Engineer might do:

1. **Testing Activities**:
   - What testing do they do?
   - When do they test?

2. **Non-Testing Activities**:
   - What else do they do?
   - Requirements review?
   - Process improvement?
   - Documentation?
   - Training?

3. **Compare**:
   - What % is testing vs. other activities?
   - Why do non-testing activities matter?

### Reflection

- How is QA broader than testing?
- What non-testing activities contribute to quality?
- How does this change your view of the QA role?

---

## Exercise 1.4: Quality Dimensions

### Task

Pick a software product (e.g., banking app, social media, game):

1. **Rate Each Dimension** (1-5 importance):
   - Functional Suitability
   - Performance Efficiency
   - Compatibility
   - Usability
   - Reliability
   - Security
   - Maintainability
   - Portability

2. **Justify Ratings**:
   - Why is each dimension important (or not)?
   - What would happen if it's poor?

3. **Testing Implications**:
   - How would you test each important dimension?
   - What testing would you prioritize?

### Reflection

- How does context determine which dimensions matter?
- Why can't you test everything equally?
- How does this help you prioritize testing?

Compare quality requirements for different software:

2. **Social Media App**:
   - What quality dimensions matter most?
   - What would be the impact of failure?
   - How strict should testing be?

   - What quality dimensions matter most?
   - What would be the impact of failure?
   - How strict should testing be?

### Reflection

- Why is there no "one size fits all" quality standard?
- How does this help you adapt your QA approach?

## Exercise 1.6: QA Engineer Role

### Task

Imagine you're a QA Engineer on a project:

1. **Your Responsibilities**:
   - What would you do daily?
   - What would you do weekly?
   - What would you do per release?

2. **Beyond Testing**:
   - How would you contribute to quality beyond testing?
   - What processes would you improve?
   - How would you advocate for quality?

3. **Success Metrics**:
   - How would you measure your success?
   - Is it just "bugs found"?
   - What else matters?

### Reflection

- How is QA Engineer role broader than "tester"?
- What makes a QA Engineer effective?
- How does this align with your career goals?
